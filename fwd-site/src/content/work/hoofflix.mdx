## The Problem

Vets, farriers, equine physiotherapists, and trainers all have one thing in common: they need Continuing Professional Development records to maintain their qualifications and demonstrate competence to clients. Most of them manage this with a spreadsheet, a folder of certificates, or nothing at all.

The general-purpose platforms — YouTube, Udemy, Teachable — were not designed for equestrian professionals. Content is mixed in with everything else, CPD tracking is non-existent, and there is no concept of professional credentials or a directory where clients can find and verify practitioners.

The equestrian industry needed a platform built for how it actually works.

## What We Built

A complete learning and professional development platform, purpose-built for the equestrian sector.

**Content for every format**: Courses, series, articles, podcasts, and webinars all in one place. Professionals can learn in the format that suits them — watching a module between appointments, listening to a podcast while driving to a yard, reading an article on the yard itself.

**CPD tracking that actually produces the records professionals need**: Every piece of completed content is logged against the user's CPD record. Professionals can export a PDF report showing completed hours, content types, and dates — the format most professional bodies require. This was not bolted on; it was a core design requirement from day one.

**Practitioner directory**: A searchable, map-based directory of equestrian professionals on the platform. Practitioners control their own profiles. Clients can search by location, specialism, and credentials. It gives professionals visibility they could not get from a general-purpose platform and gives clients a trusted way to find qualified practitioners.

**Subscription and commerce in one system**: Users can subscribe to an access tier (Free, Basic, or Pro) or purchase individual courses and products outright. A built-in shop handles both digital and physical products. Discount codes, gift vouchers, and bundle pricing all work within the same checkout flow.

**Engagement mechanics**: Streaks reward consistent learning. Achievements mark meaningful milestones. A recommendation engine surfaces relevant content based on what the user has already completed — keeping professionals engaged beyond their initial course purchase.

## The Native Apps

Hoofflix is available as a native app on iOS and Android, packaged using Capacitor. Students and professionals get the polished mobile experience they expect from a serious product. The app is listed on the App Store and Google Play.

Payments are handled through the web checkout rather than in-app purchases, which avoids Apple's 30% transaction fee. For a platform with multiple pricing tiers and one-off purchases, that is a meaningful difference to the business model.

## Why Not an Off-the-Shelf LMS

The combination of CPD tracking, practitioner directory, equestrian-specific content taxonomy, native apps, and commerce is not something any existing platform provides. Generic LMS tools handle courses. They do not handle professional credential records, searchable practitioner profiles, or the specific content types that equestrian professionals actually consume.

A custom build meant every feature could be designed for the equestrian professional audience, not adapted from something built for a different market entirely.
