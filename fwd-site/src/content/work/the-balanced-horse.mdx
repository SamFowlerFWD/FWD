## The Problem

Rebecca Duke runs The Balanced Horse, an equestrian training business. She had the content, the audience, and the expertise. What she did not have was a platform she owned.

Third-party course platforms charge a monthly subscription and take a percentage of every sale. For someone building a content business, that compounds quickly. They also come with constraints: their branding, their checkout, their terms. If the platform changes its pricing or shuts down, your business is at risk.

Rebecca needed a platform she controlled completely, one that could handle subscriptions and one-off purchases, manage course content properly, and work on mobile for students watching from yards and stables.

## What We Built

A complete video learning platform, built from scratch.

**Content structure**: Courses at the top level, each broken into modules, each module into individual lessons. Video, written guidance, and downloadable resources per lesson.

**Access model**: Students subscribe to a plan or buy a course outright. Subscriptions unlock specific course libraries. Drip rules control when lessons become available, keeping students engaged over time rather than dumping everything at once.

**Video delivery**: Adaptive streaming adjusts quality to the viewer's connection. Full HD on home broadband, a reduced resolution on a slow yard connection, both play smoothly. Every session is authenticated so content is protected.

**Two ways to buy**: Monthly subscriptions for ongoing access. One-off purchases for standalone courses or specialist topics. Discount codes for launches and promotions. Both work in the same system.

**Admin dashboard**: Rebecca manages everything herself. Upload and order content, set pricing, view analytics, manage students, create discount codes. No technical knowledge required and no need to contact us for day-to-day tasks.

## The Native App

The platform is packaged as a native app for iOS and Android. Students can download it from the App Store and Google Play for the polished experience they expect from a professional product. Payments are handled in a way that avoids Apple's 30% transaction fee, which makes a meaningful difference to margins at scale.

## Why Not Just Use an Existing Platform

Off-the-shelf platforms would cost Rebecca a few thousand pounds a year in platform fees alone, plus a percentage of every sale. Over two or three years, a custom build pays for itself and gives her something she owns outright.

There is also the flexibility argument. Teachable and Thinkific are built for a broad market. A custom build is built for how Rebecca teaches and how her students learn.
