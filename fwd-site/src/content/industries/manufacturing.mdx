## The Problem with Most Manufacturing Websites

Manufacturing businesses, especially smaller ones in rural areas, tend to get overlooked by web agencies. The result is either no website at all, or one that was built ten years ago and hasn't been touched since. Meanwhile, your competitors with a decent online presence are picking up the enquiries you should be getting.

The real issue isn't just the website itself. It's all the manual processes behind it. Product specs stored in PDFs that nobody can find. Quote requests coming in by phone and getting scribbled on paper. Customers ringing up to check order status because there's no other way to find out. All of this costs time and money.

## What We Build for Manufacturers

The starting point for most manufacturers is a proper product catalogue. Not a basic list of items, but a searchable, filterable catalogue with technical specifications, downloadable data sheets, and clear enquiry forms. Something your sales team can actually point customers to, and something that works properly on a phone.

From there, we often build quote request systems. Instead of fielding phone calls and manually typing up quotes, customers fill in what they need online, and you get a structured request you can respond to quickly. Some clients take this further with automated pricing for standard items.

Customer portals are another common build. Give your repeat buyers a login where they can view past orders, download invoices, and check lead times. It saves your team from answering the same questions over and over.

## Built for How You Actually Work

Manufacturing isn't like retail. You're not selling hundreds of items a day to consumers. Your sales cycles are longer, your products are more complex, and your relationships matter more. We build systems that reflect that reality, not generic ecommerce templates that don't fit.

Whether you're a precision engineering firm in Norfolk or a specialist fabricator anywhere in the UK, we'll build something that actually makes your day-to-day easier.
