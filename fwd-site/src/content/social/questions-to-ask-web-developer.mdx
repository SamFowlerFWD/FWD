## LinkedIn

Hiring a web developer is confusing if you have never done it before. Most people do not know what to ask, and some developers rely on that.

Here are the questions I wish every client would ask me.

**1. Can I see examples of your work?** You want live websites, not NDAs on everything.

**2. What is included in the price?** Get a specific list. Design, development, mobile responsiveness, SEO setup, browser testing, content management training. All of this should be stated clearly before any work starts.

**3. Who will actually build my website?** At an agency, the senior people in the pitch meeting may not be the ones doing the work. Know who it actually is.

**4. How long will it take?** A simple brochure site done properly takes 2-3 weeks minimum. "We can do it in 3 days" for anything beyond a basic single page means something is being cut.

**5. What happens after launch?** Websites need maintenance, security updates, and occasional fixes. Someone needs to handle that. Find out who and what it costs.

**6. Will I own the website?** You should own the code, the domain, the design, and the content. If you cannot take your site to a different developer without a full rebuild, you are locked in.

**7. How will my site show up on Google?** Basic on-page SEO should be standard in every build. Meta tags, heading structure, fast loading, mobile responsiveness. These are not optional extras in 2026.

**8. What is your process?** A developer who can explain how a project runs from brief to launch has done this before. A developer who says "we will figure it out as we go" has not.

**9. Can I update content myself?** If changing a phone number requires a developer, the site is not built in your interest.

**10. What if I am not happy?** Know the revision policy before you commit.

And the bonus question most people forget: "What would you recommend for my budget?" A good developer will tell you what is essential, what is nice to have, and what can wait.
