## LinkedIn

I am a freelancer, so you would expect me to argue for that side.

I will try to be fair. There are situations where an agency is genuinely the right call, and I will say so.

Here is the honest comparison.

**Freelancer:** £799-£5,000, 2-6 weeks, direct communication with the person building your site, high flexibility, 2-4 active projects at any time.

**Agency:** £5,000-£20,000+, 6-16 weeks, account manager as your main contact, process-driven, 20-30 active projects at any time.

A freelancer makes sense when your project is straightforward, when budget matters, and when you want to talk directly to the person writing the code. When you email a freelancer, you are emailing the developer. There is no game of telephone through account managers and project managers.

An agency makes sense when you genuinely need multiple specialists working simultaneously, when your project requires enterprise infrastructure, or when compliance requirements demand a vendor of a certain size.

The downsides of freelancers are real too. Capacity is limited. If I am fully booked, you wait. There is no colleague to pick up the work if something goes wrong. And one person genuinely cannot be an expert at everything.

The downsides of agencies are also worth naming. Agency quotes are higher because you are covering office rent, salaries for non-billable staff, layers of management, and profit margins. When an agency charges £10,000 for a website, the developer doing the actual work might be spending the same number of hours as a freelancer charging £3,000. You are paying for infrastructure you do not benefit from.

The questions to ask either option: Can I see your portfolio? What is included in the price? Who will actually build my site? What happens after launch? Do I own the code?
