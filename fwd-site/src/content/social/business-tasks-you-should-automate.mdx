## LinkedIn

You spend Monday morning chasing invoices. Tuesday copying data between spreadsheets. Wednesday sending the same email to 15 customers with slightly different details.

These are the tasks that eat hours every week but never feel urgent enough to fix.

Here are five tasks that almost every small UK business can automate, with real costs and honest assessments of whether it's worth it.

**1. Invoice generation and payment reminders.** Your invoices should send themselves, with automatic follow-up emails at 7, 14, and 30 days. Cost to set up: £799-£1,500. Time saved: 3-5 hours per week.

**2. Customer follow-ups.** Most businesses intend to follow up after every job. Most forget at least half the time. Automate it and every customer gets a personalised email at the right moment, every single time.

**3. Appointment booking.** The back-and-forth of scheduling is entirely avoidable. Customers pick a slot from your real availability, get an automatic confirmation, and a reminder the day before. No-shows drop by 40-60%.

**4. Data entry between systems.** If you copy the same information from one tool into another more than a few times a week, you can automate that. One client had 500 eBay listings that needed migrating to Shopify. A script handled the whole lot in seconds.

**5. Report generation.** Weekly reports that pull from multiple tools and arrive in your inbox without anyone touching a spreadsheet.

The ROI calculation is simple. If automation costs £1,000 and saves 3 hours a week, and your time is worth £30 an hour, it pays for itself in about 11 weeks. After that, it is pure saving.

One thing worth saying clearly: ChatGPT is not automation. You still have to open it, paste in your data, write a prompt, and copy the output somewhere useful. That is a tool, not a system.

Real automation runs without anyone being there. No one needs to remember. No one needs to copy and paste.
