## LinkedIn

"How much does a website cost?" is the question I get asked more than any other.

The honest answer: it depends. But unlike most developers, I am going to tell you exactly what "it depends" means.

Here is what you should actually expect to pay in the UK in 2026.

**DIY (Wix, Squarespace):** £0-£50/month. Fine for personal projects or testing an idea. Not where you want to stay long-term if you are serious about the business.

**Budget freelancer:** £200-£500. Usually a WordPress theme with your logo dropped in. Can look fine at first glance, often slow, poorly maintained, and a nightmare to update.

**Professional freelancer:** £799-£5,000. This is where most small businesses should be looking. Custom design, proper SEO foundations, built for your specific requirements.

**Agency:** £5,000-£20,000+. Warranted for large, complex projects that genuinely need a team. For most small business sites, you are paying for overhead you do not benefit from.

The hidden costs nobody tells you about: hosting (£5-£150/month), domain name (£10-£15/year), email (£0-£6/user/month), and ongoing content updates if you cannot manage them yourself.

The reason some developers are very cheap: they are installing a theme, changing the colours, and dropping in your logo. The "design" is someone else's template. These sites tend to be slow, bloated, and a problem to maintain.

The reason agencies are expensive: their quotes cover office rent, salaries for non-billable staff, layers of management, and profit margins. When an agency charges £10,000, the developer doing the work might be spending the same hours as a freelancer charging £3,000.
