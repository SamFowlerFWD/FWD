## 1. Voice Profile

**Tone:** Confident, direct, warm. Sam talks to clients the way a knowledgeable friend would: no fluff, no jargon, no overselling. He's the person who says "honestly, you don't need that" when a simpler solution works better.

**Register:** Professional but not corporate. Think pub conversation with someone who really knows their stuff, not a boardroom pitch deck.

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: build, create, sort out, set up, help, save, practical, honest, straightforward, work smarter
- Avoid: leverage, harness, synergy, navigate, empower, cutting-edge, world-class, seamless, in today's digital landscape, it's worth noting

**Personality traits:**
- Straight-talking (says what needs saying, including "you don't need this")
- Practical (solutions over theory)
- Approachable (no one feels stupid asking questions)
- Quietly confident (lets the work speak; doesn't oversell)
- Genuinely helpful (gives useful advice even before someone becomes a client)

**How FWD sounds different from competitors:**
- Most web agencies sound corporate and interchangeable. FWD sounds like a real person.
- Budget shops sound transactional. FWD sounds invested in the outcome.
- Enterprise agencies sound impressive but distant. FWD sounds like someone you'd actually want to work with.

## 3. Blog Strategy: The Content Engine

### Content Pillars

The blog serves three functions: drive organic traffic, build topical authority, and provide social proof. Content falls into four categories:

**Pillar 1: Practical Guides** (top of funnel, informational intent)
Target audience: people researching before they buy. These articles answer the questions your potential clients type into Google.

**Pillar 2: Case Studies** (mid-funnel, commercial intent)
Target audience: people evaluating options. These prove you can deliver results in their specific situation.

**Pillar 3: Automation & AI Insights** (top of funnel, low competition)
Target audience: business owners curious about automation. This is your biggest content gap in the market; almost nobody writes practical automation content for UK SMBs at accessible price points.

**Pillar 4: Opinion & Expertise** (authority building)
Target audience: industry peers and sophisticated buyers. Shorter pieces that demonstrate your perspective and expertise.

## 6. Image and Visual Notes

**Homepage hero:** Dark gradient background (keep current approach), consider adding a subtle device mockup showing a real project

**Service pages:** Each should have at least one visual showing real output (screenshot, diagram, or interface mockup)

**Case studies:** Before/after comparisons where applicable, screenshots of the finished product, any metrics visualised as simple graphics

**Industry pages:** Lightweight; could use a relevant icon or illustration rather than photography to keep page weight low

**Blog posts:** Featured image for each post (OG sharing), at least one supporting image within longer articles

**All images need:**
- Descriptive alt text (not "image1.jpg")
- WebP format with fallback
- Explicit dimensions for CLS prevention
- Lazy loading below the fold
